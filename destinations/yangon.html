About Yangon

Yangon, formerly known as Rangoon, is the largest city in Myanmar and a vibrant hub of culture, history, and spirituality. Known for its colonial architecture, golden pagodas, and bustling markets, the city blends tradition with modernity.

Key Attractions

  • Shwedagon Pagoda – Myanmar's most sacred Buddhist pagoda
  • Sule Pagoda – Iconic landmark in the heart of the city
  • Kandawgyi Lake – Scenic park with a royal barge replica
  • Bogyoke Aung San Market – Colonial-era market for local goods
  • Yangon Circular Train – A local experience offering views of daily life

Best Time to Visit

The best time to visit Yangon is from November to February when the weather is cooler and less humid. These months offer comfortable conditions for sightseeing and outdoor activities.

Local Cuisine

Yangon is a food lover’s paradise with a variety of unique Burmese flavors:

  • Mohinga – Rice noodle and fish soup, the national dish
  • Lahpet Thoke – Fermented tea leaf salad
  • Shan Noodles – Tasty flat noodles with chicken or pork
  • Mont Lin Ma Yar – Savory street-side snacks
  • Coconut Noodles – Rich and creamy with local spices

Quick Facts

  • Country: Myanmar
  • Language: Burmese
  • Currency: Myanmar Kyat (MMK)
  • Time Zone: MMT (UTC +6:30)
