About Bagan

Bagan, an ancient city located on the banks of the Irrawaddy River, is one of the most remarkable archaeological sites in Southeast Asia. With thousands of temples and pagodas stretching across the plains, Bagan offers a breathtaking glimpse into Myanmar's rich Buddhist heritage.

Key Attractions

  • Ananda Temple – A beautifully preserved temple with stunning architecture
  • Shwesandaw Pagoda – Famous for panoramic sunrise and sunset views
  • Dhammayangyi Temple – The largest temple known for its brickwork
  • Hot Air Balloon Ride – A magical way to see the temples from above
  • Bagan Archaeological Museum – Insight into the city's ancient history

Best Time to Visit

The ideal time to visit Bagan is between November and February when the weather is dry and pleasant. The clear skies during these months are perfect for sightseeing and balloon rides.

Local Cuisine

Bagan offers a taste of authentic Burmese and regional cuisine:

  • Mont Hin Gar – Traditional fish broth noodle soup
  • Thanaka Chicken – Grilled chicken marinated with thanaka paste
  • Tofu Nway – Warm Shan-style tofu dish
  • Vegetarian curries – A popular option for temple-area diners
  • Tamarind Flakes – A sweet-and-sour local specialty

Quick Facts

  • Currency: Myanmar Kyat (MMK)
  • Time Zone: MMT (UTC +6:30)
